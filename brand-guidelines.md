# SuperGP - Brand Guidelines

> Version 1.1 | April 2026 | Source of truth for marketing, product, and design

---

## 1. Brand foundation

### Purpose
SuperGP exists to remove operational chaos in GP practices so teams can spend more time on patient care and less time on admin.

### Vision
A healthcare system where every GP practice runs on one modern operating system: compliance-ready, operationally clear, and clinically focused.

### Mission
Replace spreadsheets, fragmented tools, and manual workarounds with one trusted platform designed by NHS clinicians and built by experienced technology leaders.

### Values
1. **Clinical empathy**  
   We build from frontline experience, not assumptions.
2. **Operational clarity**  
   We make complex daily operations visible and manageable.
3. **Dependable execution**  
   Practice teams can rely on SuperGP every day, across every site.
4. **Straight-talking Expertsise**  
   We communicate clearly, back claims with evidence, and avoid hype.

### Brand personality
- **Direct**: clear statements, no corporate filler.
- **Credible**: specific, evidence-led, practical.
- **Empathetic**: understands stress and workload pressure.
- **Modern**: clear break from legacy systems and outdated workflows.
- **Reliable**: calm, trustworthy, and consistently useful.

### Brand promise
Less admin. More patient care.

## 2. Positioning

### Target audiences
- **Primary**: Practice Managers and Operations Leads in UK GP practices.
- **Secondary**: GP Partners, Clinical Directors, and PCN leaders.
- **Tertiary**: Multi-site decision makers evaluating operational software.

### Market problem
Most practices still run core operations through spreadsheets, messages, and disconnected systems. This creates rota instability, compliance risk, unused capacity, and avoidable admin load.

### Differentiation
SuperGP is built by working NHS GPs and experienced product and engineering leaders. It combines clinical understanding with modern software execution in one platform.

### Positioning statement
For UK GP practices under operational pressure, SuperGP is the modern practice OS that replaces fragmented tools with one connected system, giving teams visibility, control, and confidence.

### Brand pillars
1. **Built for real practice operations**  
2. **One system across people, sites, and compliance**  
3. **Audit-ready by default**  
4. **Scales from one site to many**

## 3. Messaging architecture

### Core lines
- **Primary headline**: Run your practice, without the chaos.
- **Sub-headline**: Less admin. More patient care.
- **Founder credibility line**: Designed by GPs. Engineered by Experts.

### Value proposition
SuperGP replaces spreadsheets and disconnected admin tools with one platform for rota planning, capacity visibility, task execution, and audit-ready compliance.

### Proof points and approved usage
Use quantitative claims when context allows and evidence is available:
- **Admin reduction**: "Cut admin by up to 70%."
- **Operational waste**: "15-20% of appointment slots often go unused without clear staffing visibility."
- **Cost pressure**: "Spreadsheet-led rota management can contribute to significant locum overspend."
- **Audit confidence**: "Move from reactive evidence gathering to always-on audit readiness."

### Audience message blocks
- **Practice managers**: "Stop firefighting rota gaps and task bottlenecks. SuperGP gives you one command centre for daily operations."
- **GP partners**: "Reduce admin load so clinicians and support teams can focus on patient demand."
- **PCN and multi-site leads**: "Plan, coordinate, and monitor staffing and capacity across every site in one system."

### Feature message hierarchy
Present features in this order for most sales and marketing surfaces:
1. **Smart Rota Planner** - scheduling stability, conflict prevention, faster publishing.
2. **Dashboard** - real-time visibility of staffing, rooms, tasks, and capacity.
3. **Task Manager** - repeatable workflows and traceable execution.
4. **Sites Manager** - multi-site control, scaling, and facility planning.
5. **Teams Manager** - unified workforce data, role and permission control.
6. **Policy GPT** - private, grounded SOP retrieval with ring-fenced data.

## 4. Voice and tone

### Voice characteristics
- **Direct**: lead with the problem or outcome in the first line.
- **Empathetic**: acknowledge pressure points before presenting solutions.
- **Confident**: specific claims, practical wording, no exaggeration.
- **Action-oriented**: use active verbs and clear next steps.

### Tone by context
- **Marketing headlines**: bold, punchy, challenge-led.
- **Feature pages**: clear, benefit-first, operationally specific.
- **Product UX copy**: calm, concise, instructional.
- **Support and onboarding**: reassuring, practical, never blaming.
- **Compliance content**: precise, plain English, fully auditable.

### Writing standards
- Use UK English (`practice`, `organise`, `licence` where applicable).
- Prefer active voice and short-to-medium sentences.
- Use numerals for stats and impact claims.
- Spell out acronyms on first use (for example, Primary Care Network (PCN)).

### Approved and avoided language
- **Use more of**: `in one place`, `audit-ready`, `in seconds`, `real-time visibility`, `reduce admin`.
- **Use less of**: `revolutionary`, `seamless`, `game-changing`, `best-in-class`, `leverage`.
- **Never use**: vague hype with no practical outcome.

### Do and do not examples
- **Do**: "Your rota should not collapse every week. SuperGP flags conflicts before you publish."
- **Do not**: "Our innovative platform leverages modern technology for workforce optimisation."
- **Do**: "Manage all sites from one dashboard."
- **Do not**: "Facilitate cross-location operational synergies."

## 6. Visual identity direction

### Logo system
- Required lockups: primary horizontal wordmark, stacked variant, icon-only mark.
- Clear space: minimum equals the height of the `G` in the wordmark on all sides.
- Minimum digital size: 120px width for the full wordmark.
- Do not stretch, recolour outside palette, or place on busy backgrounds without contrast support.

### Colour palette

SuperGP uses a **dark-first, electric palette**. The brand must feel unmistakably tech-forward — a clear visual break from NHS-blue conventions and legacy software aesthetics.

**Brand colours:**
| Name | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Electric Indigo | `#5B4FFF` | Primary — CTAs, interactive elements, focus rings |
| Electric Cyan | `#00D9F7` | Accent — gradient tail, info states, links |
| Electric Lime | `#A8FF3E` | Success — positive metrics, health outcomes |
| Soft Violet | `#8B5CF6` | AI features — Policy GPT, depth accents |

**Signature gradient:**
`linear-gradient(135deg, #5B4FFF 0%, #00D9F7 100%)`
Used on: primary CTAs, hero headline text spans, key accent elements.

**Semantic colours:**
| Name | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Warm Amber | `#FFB830` | Warnings, pending states |
| Hot Red | `#FF4D6A` | Errors, critical alerts |

**Dark surfaces (default UI):**
| Name | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| BG | `#070B14` | Deepest background |
| Surface 1 | `#0D1422` | Card backgrounds |
| Surface 2 | `#121929` | Elevated panels |
| Surface 3 | `#1A2236` | Hover states |

**Text on dark:**
- Primary text: `#F0F4FF`
- Secondary text: `rgba(240,244,255,0.55)`
- Muted/labels: `rgba(240,244,255,0.30)`

**Colour rules:**
- Never use NHS blue (`#005EB8`) or generic corporate blues as primary brand colours.
- Electric Lime replaces conventional green for success states — it is intentionally bold.
- Glow effects (`box-shadow` with brand colour at low opacity) are on-brand for primary interactive elements.

### Typography
- **Primary typeface**: Inter (400, 500, 600, 700)
- **Monospace support typeface**: JetBrains Mono
- Use sentence case for headlines and labels.
- Keep body copy readable and concise; target 60-72 characters per line in long-form contexts.

### Imagery and iconography
- Use authentic clinical and operational contexts over generic stock imagery.
- Prefer product-led visuals where possible (UI, workflow states, dashboards).
- Keep icon style consistent (outline style, uniform stroke weight).

### Accessibility standard
All production usage must meet WCAG AA contrast requirements (minimum 4.5:1 for normal text).

## 7. Brand protection and governance

### Legal and naming controls
- Protect `SuperGP` as a word mark in relevant UK classes and territories.
- Use approved product naming consistently: `SuperGP`, `Smart Rota Planner`, `Policy GPT`.
- Do not permit third-party modification of logos, names, or taglines.

### Partner and press usage
- Maintain a controlled press kit with approved logos, screenshots, and boilerplate copy.
- Require review before partner pages, marketplace listings, and event collateral go live.
- Use "Works with SuperGP" for integrations rather than substituting the core brand mark.

### Brand compliance checklist
Before publishing any customer-facing asset, confirm:
- [ ] Correct logo lockup and safe area used.
- [ ] Approved colour and type system used.
- [ ] UK English and SuperGP voice standards applied.
- [ ] Messaging aligns to approved positioning and proof points.
- [ ] Accessibility checks completed.
- [ ] Legal and privacy claims reviewed where relevant.

### Governance cadence
- **Monthly**: copy and campaign spot checks across web, product, and sales collateral.
- **Quarterly**: full brand consistency review and message performance assessment.
- **Biannually**: strategic refresh of messaging priorities and proof points.
- **Owner**: Product/Brand lead with sign-off from Clinical and Technology leadership.

### Escalation rule
Any new tagline, major claim, or visual identity change must be reviewed before public release.

## 8. Quick implementation guide

### Priority rollout sequence
1. Align homepage, product marketing pages, and demo deck to this guide.
2. Standardise in-product key copy surfaces (navigation labels, onboarding, alerts).
3. Update partner and press materials with approved language and visuals.

### Definition of done
Brand implementation is complete when all primary channels use approved messaging, visual standards, and governance checks from this document.
